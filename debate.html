Advanced Techniques

The Socratic Method

Ask probing questions that expose weaknesses in your opponent's logic. Lead them to contradict themselves or reveal gaps in their reasoning.

"If you believe X is true, then how do you explain Y? Doesn't this contradict your earlier statement about Z?"

Reframing

Present the issue from a different perspective that favors your position. Change the context or scope of the discussion strategically.

"While my opponent focuses on short-term costs, let's consider the long-term benefits to society..."

Analogies and Metaphors

Use vivid comparisons to make complex ideas more understandable and memorable. Choose analogies that support your argument.

"Implementing this policy without proper safeguards is like performing surgery without anesthesia."

Concede and Redirect

Acknowledge valid points from your opponent while redirecting to stronger ground. This shows reasonableness while maintaining your position.

"I agree that cost is a concern, but we must also consider the human cost of inaction..."

The Burden of Proof

Understand who needs to prove what. If your opponent makes a claim, they must provide evidence. Don't let them shift the burden to you unfairly.

"You've made a strong assertion. What evidence do you have to support this claim?"
